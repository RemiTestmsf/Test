BASICS: ENERGY

BASIC 1

Individuals are protected against electrocution and fire. Devices are protected against fire, power stability, and effects of lightning.

Best Practices

  • All live cables must be inaccessible. Refer to Rules & Tools p. 111.
  • The neutral and canopy of all generators must be grounded. Refer to Rules & Tools p. 81 and 191.
  • All sockets must be grounded. Refer to Rules & Tools p. 159.
  • There is 300-mA Residual Current Device (RCD) protection on all general circuits. Refer to Rules & Tools p. 93.
  • There is 30-mA Residual Current Device (RCD) on all final circuits. Refer to Rules & Tools p. 93.
  • Miniature Circuit Breakers (MCB) and electrical wires are properly scaled. Refer to Rules & Tools p.149.
  • There is an ABC type fire extinguisher of dry powder close to every distribution board and generator. They are updated in the maintenance plan.

BASIC 2

Services are protected against breakdown, failure of power sources, or effects of any other interruption.

Best Practices

  • Sensitive equipment must be protected against voltage and frequency variations. Refer to the Equipment Protection Table.
  • Critical zones/wards (e.g., OT and ICU) are equipped with backup systems (UPS). Refer to UPS installation requirements.
  • The power assessment and consumption measurements are made regularly.
  • Generators and UPS systems are scaled based on the power requirements assessment. Refer to Power assessment tools.

Monitoring

  • Facilities are visited by an MSF electrician 6 months after opening and every 18 months thereafter.
  • A power requirement assessment is carried out every 6 months.

Benefits

  • Users and facilities are safe from electrocution due to direct contact, insulation faults, or bad grounding.
  • Fires do not break out due to overloads or short circuits.
  • Ensures the continuity of medical and non-medical activities.
  • No damaged equipment.

Common Mistakes

  • Connections are spliced (connection without electrical connectors).
  • There is no junction box.
  • Grounding is not installed.
  • Live wires are left without mechanical protection (trunking, pipe, etc.).
  • The generator is grounded but the neutral is not bonded.
  • RCD protection is 300 mA instead of 30 mA for sensitive circuits.
  • Non-waterproof fittings (junction boxes, plugs, light fixtures, etc.) are installed outdoors.
  • The person doing electrical work is not an electrician trained in MSF standards.
  • No maintenance plan in place.

Roles and Responsibilities

  • The Log is responsible for the assessment of the installation and for deciding if electrical work is needed.
  • If the Log is not qualified enough to carry out the electrical work, an electrician must be contacted.
  • The Log must ensure that proper assessments are carried out on time by qualified electricians.
  • The Log is responsible for ensuring energy service continuity and proper backup.
  • If the Log is not experienced enough to ensure service continuity, an electrician must be contacted.

Necessary Resources

Time

After assessment (~1 week), technical design (~1 week), and procurement (from 1 week to 3 months), it can take around 10 to 25 days to bring a 100mÂ² facility up to standard.

Supply

Local suppliers must be validated according to the local purchasing procedure. If quality cannot be ensured, international purchase is preferred. Watch out for counterfeit items!

HR (Human Resources)

  • Level 9, Energy Manager: Design, implementation, and coordination of complex and/or large energy projects.
  • Level 8, Energy Specialist: Ensures the correct management and control of electrical installations.
  • Level 5, Electrician: Performs preventive and corrective maintenance of electrical equipment and installation.

Equipment

Essential items include: RCD, RCD tester, wire gauge, voltmeter, junction boxes, rapid connectors, terminal blocks, circuit breakers, grounding rods, electrician tool bags, and UPS systems.
