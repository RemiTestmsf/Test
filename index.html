Key Safety Practices

  • All live cables must be inaccessible. Refer to Rules & Tools p. 111.
  • The neutral and canopy of all generators must be grounded. Refer to Rules & Tools p. 81 and 191.
  • All sockets must be grounded. Refer to Rules & Tools p. 159.
  • There is 300-mA Residual Current Device (RCD) protection on all general circuits. Refer to Rules & Tools p. 93.
  • There is 30-mA Residual Current Device (RCD) on all final circuits. Refer to Rules & Tools p. 93.
  • Miniature Circuit Breakers (MCB) and electrical wires are properly scaled. Refer to Rules & Tools p.149.
  • There is an ABC type fire extinguisher of dry powder close to every distribution board and generator. They are updated in the maintenance plan.
  • Sensitive equipment must be protected against voltage and frequency variations. Refer to the Equipment Protection Table.
  • Critical zones/wards (e.g. OT and ICU) are equipped with back-up systems (UPS). Refer to UPS installation requirements.
  • The power assessment and consumption measurements are made regularly.
  • Generators and UPS systems are scaled based on the power requirements assessment. Refer to Power assessment tools.
  • Facilities are visited by an electrician every 6 to 18 months.
  • A power requirement assessment is carried out every 6 months.
  • Proper grounding must be installed to prevent electrocution risks.

Common Mistakes

  • Splicing connections without electrical connectors.
  • Not installing proper grounding.
  • Leaving live wires unprotected.
  • Using non-waterproof fittings outdoors.
  • Not having a maintenance plan.

Roles & Responsibilities

The logistics team is responsible for ensuring safe electrical installations. If they lack expertise, a certified electrician should be hired.
